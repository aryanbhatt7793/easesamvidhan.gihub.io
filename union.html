Chapter IV: The Union Judiciary

The Supreme Court is the highest court in India. It solves major issues, like disputes between states or between the government and states. It also hears appeals in big cases and can review its decisions.

The Chief Justice leads the Supreme Court and can appoint extra judges when needed. The decisions made by the Supreme Court are final and must be followed by all courts in India. The President can also ask the Supreme Court for advice on difficult legal issues.
